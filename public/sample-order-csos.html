Understanding CS(OS) - Civil Suit (Original Side)

A Civil Suit (Original Side) is a lawsuit of a civil nature filed directly in the High Court, which is acting as the court of first instance (i.e., its 'Original Jurisdiction'). This typically happens for cases of a specific nature or high pecuniary value, as defined by law. This case, involving a claim for money and damages, is a classic example of a commercial dispute handled by the High Court's Original Side.

Orders of the Court

Title: Application for summary judgment | Type: order | Date: 15-07-2024

The application filed by the Plaintiff under Order XIIIA of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, for summary judgment is hereby dismissed. The court is of the view that the Defendant has raised triable issues that require leading of evidence. The case will proceed to trial.

Purpose of the Order: A summary judgment is a shortcut to a final decision without a full trial. The Plaintiff likely argued that the Defendant had no real defense. By dismissing this application, the court has decided that the Defendant's arguments have merit and deserve a full hearing with witnesses and evidence. This ensures a fair trial.

Title: Summons issued to defendant | Type: notice | Date: 10-05-2024

Summons to be served upon the Defendant. The Defendant is directed to file a written statement within 30 days of the receipt of the summons. The summons shall indicate that if the defendant fails to appear, the suit may be heard and decided ex-parte.

Purpose of the Order: This is the formal notification to the defendant that they are being sued. The summons legally obligates the defendant to appear in court and respond to the plaintiff's claims. The direction to file a "written statement" is the instruction to provide a formal, point-by-point reply to the allegations made in the suit.
