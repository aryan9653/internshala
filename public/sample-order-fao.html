Understanding a First Appeal from Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is a type of appeal filed in a High Court against specific orders passed by a lower court (like a District Court) during the course of a lawsuit, even before the final judgment is delivered. These are not appeals against the final verdict, but against interim (temporary) orders. For example, an order granting or refusing a temporary injunction can be challenged through an FAO.
