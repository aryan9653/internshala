Understanding FAO - First Appeal from Order

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed in the High Court against an order passed by a lower court (like a District Court) during the course of a lawsuit, i.e., before the final judgment. These appeals typically challenge interim orders, such as an order granting or refusing a temporary injunction. The 'Appellant' is the party who is aggrieved by the lower court's order and files the appeal, while the 'Respondent' is the other party.
