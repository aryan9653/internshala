About This Case Type

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed against an interim or preliminary order of a lower court, not against a final judgment. These orders are passed during the pendency of a suit to address urgent matters. A common example is an appeal against an order granting or refusing a temporary injunction. The purpose of an FAO is to get a quick review of an interim order that could significantly affect a party's rights before the main case is decided. In this mock case, the appeal challenges an ad-interim injunction order in a commercial dispute.

Orders of the Court

Stay on lower court order

Type: order | Date: 25-07-2024

The operation of the impugned order of the trial court dated [date] is stayed till the next date of hearing. The respondent is restrained from taking any coercive action against the appellant based on the said order.

Purpose of the Order: A stay order temporarily freezes the effect of the lower court's decision. This is a significant relief for the appellant, as it prevents immediate harm while the High Court examines the correctness of the interim injunction. It suggests the High Court sees a prima facie case (a strong initial argument) in favor of the appellant and believes that maintaining the status quo is necessary to prevent injustice.

Notice of appeal

Type: notice | Date: 20-06-2024

Issue notice of the appeal to the respondent, returnable on the next date. The appellant is directed to provide a complete set of the appeal paper book to the respondent's counsel within one week.

Purpose of the Order: This is the first step in any appeal. The court formally notifies the opposing party (the respondent) that an appeal has been filed against them. This ensures that the principles of natural justice are followed, giving the respondent a fair opportunity to appear and defend the lower court's order.
