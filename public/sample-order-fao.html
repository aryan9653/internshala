Understanding a First Appeal from Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed in the High Court against an order passed by a lower court (like a District or Civil Judge). Unlike an appeal against a final judgment, an FAO challenges an interim or temporary order made during the course of a lawsuit. Common examples include orders granting or refusing a temporary injunction, which are challenged to prevent immediate harm while the main case is still pending.
