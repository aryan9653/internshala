Understanding First Appeals From Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed against an 'interim' or 'interlocutory' order passed by a lower court during a pending suit. Unlike an appeal against a final judgment, an FAO challenges a specific procedural or temporary decision made by the judge before the main case is decided. Common examples include orders granting or refusing a temporary injunction.
