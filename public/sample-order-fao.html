Understanding First Appeals from Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed against an interim (temporary) order passed by a lower court during the pendency of a suit. It is not an appeal against the final judgment. These appeals are crucial in civil litigation, especially in cases involving intellectual property, property disputes, or contracts, where a temporary injunction can significantly impact the parties. The appeal in this case challenges an "ad-interim injunction," which is a temporary order granted to protect a party's rights until the main case is decided.
