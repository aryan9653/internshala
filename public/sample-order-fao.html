About First Appeal from Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed against an interim or procedural order passed by a lower court (like a District Court) during a pending suit. Unlike a regular appeal that challenges a final judgment, an FAO challenges a specific, intermediate decision. Common examples include appeals against orders granting or refusing a temporary injunction. This mechanism allows parties to seek a quick review of significant interim orders without waiting for the entire case to conclude, which could take years.
