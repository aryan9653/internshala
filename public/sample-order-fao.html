Understanding a First Appeal from Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed in a High Court against an interim or temporary order passed by a lower court during a pending suit. It does not challenge the final judgment but rather a specific, intermediate decision. Common examples include appeals against orders granting or refusing a temporary injunction, which is an order to prevent a party from doing a certain act until the case is finally decided.
