Understanding a First Appeal from Order (FAO)

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is an appeal filed against an interim or procedural order of a lower court, rather than against the final judgment. These appeals are crucial for challenging decisions made during the course of a lawsuit, such as an order granting or refusing a temporary injunction, which could have a significant impact on the parties before the case is fully decided.
