What is a First Appeal from Order (FAO)?

A First Appeal from Order (FAO) is a type of appeal filed in a higher court to challenge an interim or temporary order passed by a lower court during a pending lawsuit. Unlike a regular appeal that challenges the final judgment, an FAO specifically targets orders that are not final but have significant consequences for the parties involved. A common example is appealing an order granting or refusing a temporary injunction. This allows a party who is aggrieved by an interim order to seek immediate review from a higher court without having to wait for the entire case to conclude.
