Understanding a Writ Petition (Civil) - W.P.(C)

A Writ Petition (Civil) is a formal request filed in a High Court or the Supreme Court asking the court to issue a "writ" â€“ a formal written order. These are typically used to enforce fundamental rights guaranteed by the Constitution. In a civil context, they are often filed against government bodies or public officials to challenge their actions, seek remedies for rights violations, or compel them to perform their legal duties. Public Interest Litigations (PILs) are a common form of writ petitions.
