Understanding a Writ Petition (Civil) - W.P.(C)

A Writ Petition is a formal written order from a court. When filed under the "Civil" category, it is used by citizens to enforce their fundamental rights against the state or any government authority. The High Court is asked to intervene when a government body has acted unlawfully or failed to perform its legal duty, impacting a citizen's rights. Common writs include Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Certiorari, Prohibition, and Quo Warranto.
