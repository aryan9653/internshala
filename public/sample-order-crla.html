What is a Criminal Appeal (CRL.A)?

A Criminal Appeal (CRL.A) is a legal proceeding initiated by a person convicted of a crime to have their case reviewed by a higher court. The convicted person (the "appellant") argues that the trial court made a legal error that led to an incorrect judgment. The appeal is not a new trial; rather, the appellate court reviews the record of the trial court proceedings to determine if any reversible errors were made. The State can also file an appeal to challenge an acquittal or to argue for a more severe sentence. In this case, the appeal is against a conviction under Section 302 of the Indian Penal Code (IPC), which deals with the punishment for murder.
