Understanding Criminal Appeals (CRL.A)

A Criminal Appeal (CRL.A) is filed in a higher court to challenge the judgment or order of a lower court in a criminal case. This can be an appeal against a conviction, where the accused has been found guilty and sentenced, or an appeal against an acquittal, where the accused has been found not guilty. The primary purpose is to review the lower court's decision for errors of law or fact. In this case, the appeal is against a conviction for murder (Section 302 of the Indian Penal Code), which is one of the most serious offenses, carrying a minimum sentence of life imprisonment.
