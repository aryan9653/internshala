Understanding a Criminal Appeal (CRL.A)

A Criminal Appeal is filed in a higher court to challenge the judgment of a lower court in a criminal case. This can be an appeal against a conviction (where the accused is found guilty) or against an acquittal (where the accused is found not guilty). The appellate court reviews the evidence and legal arguments from the original trial to determine if the lower court's decision was correct.
