Understanding Criminal Appeals (CRL.A)

A Criminal Appeal (CRL.A) is filed in a higher court to challenge the judgment or order of a lower court in a criminal case. This can be an appeal against a conviction (where the accused has been found guilty) or against an acquittal (where the accused has been found not guilty). The primary objective is to review the lower court's decision for errors in law or fact.
