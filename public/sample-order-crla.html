Understanding CRL.A - Criminal Appeal

A Criminal Appeal is filed by a person convicted of a crime by a lower court (e.g., a Sessions Court). The appeal challenges the correctness of the judgment of conviction and/or the sentence imposed. In serious cases, such as those under Section 302 (murder) of the Indian Penal Code, the appeal lies before the High Court. The convicted person is the 'Appellant', and the 'State' is the 'Respondent' that prosecuted the case in the trial court.
