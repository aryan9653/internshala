About Criminal Appeal (CRL.A)

A Criminal Appeal is a legal proceeding initiated to challenge the judgment or order of a lower court in a criminal case. The appellant, who is typically the convicted person, argues that the trial court made a legal or factual error, leading to a wrongful conviction or an unjust sentence. The appellate court (in this case, the High Court) reviews the trial court's records, evidence, and legal arguments to determine if the decision was correct. The appeal is a crucial safeguard in the justice system to protect against miscarriages of justice.
