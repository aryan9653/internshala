Understanding a Criminal Appeal (CRL.A)

A Criminal Appeal (CRL.A) is a legal proceeding initiated in a higher court to review the decision of a lower court in a criminal case. This typically happens when one of the parties—either the convicted person (the accused) or the state (the prosecution)—is dissatisfied with the judgment, sentence, or order passed by the trial court. The primary purpose is to rectify errors of law or fact that may have occurred during the trial.
