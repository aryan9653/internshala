Understanding a Criminal Appeal (CRL.A)

A Criminal Appeal is filed in the High Court to challenge the judgment of a lower criminal court (like a Sessions Court). This can be filed by a person who has been convicted of a crime, seeking to have their conviction overturned or their sentence reduced. The State can also file an appeal to seek a more severe punishment or to challenge an acquittal. These appeals involve a re-examination of the evidence and legal arguments from the original trial.
